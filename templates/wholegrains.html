Whole Grains

Most people rely on grains as a staple food. Therefore, it is important to know the difference between whole and refined grains. Incorporating whole grains into your daily diet can help maintain health. Whole grains contain vitamins, minerals, healthy fats and dietary fiber that are not found in refined grains. They can help lower blood sugar and cholesterol levels, as well as maintain a healthy digestive system.

Refined grains: White rice, white colored noodles (such as flat rice noodles and rice vermicelli), white bread, regular pasta
Whole grains: Brown rice, red rice, quinoa, millet, buckwheat soba noodles, whole wheat pasta

Figure source: Harvard TH Chan School of Public Health


Tips for eating more whole grains:

  • Add red rice, brown rice, quinoa, millet or oatmeal when you cook white rice.
  • Start from adding one handful of whole grains to refined grains. Slowly increase the amount of whole grains added until reaching at least a 1:1 ratio.
  • Have oatmeal for breakfast.
  • Choose whole grain bread over white bread.
  • If you like noodles, try soba/buckwheat noodles.
  • Have popcorn as snacks (be careful of added salt and sugar).
  • When increasing intake of whole grains, remember to drink more water to facilitate digestion.
